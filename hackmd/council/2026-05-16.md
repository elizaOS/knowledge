## Key Points for Deliberation

### 1. Topic: AI-Native Economic Primitives

**Summary of Topic:** Emergence of 'Pay or Burn' escrow protocols and autonomous mining integrations (GODL) suggests a trend toward agents managing their own fiscal risk without human arbitration.

#### Deliberation Items (Questions):

**Question 1:** Should the ElizaOS core framework adopt the 'Pay or Burn' mechanism as the native standard for agent-to-agent transactions?

  **Context:**
  - `ReaWorks introduced an escrow primitive on Base for AI-agent work that minimizes trust logic.`
  - `Novel protocol implements Pay or Burn to remove human arbitration via game theory.`

  **Multiple Choice Answers:**
    a) Adopt as Core Standard
        *Implication:* Positions ElizaOS as the leader in trustless AI-to-AI commerce but risks high capital loss in early debugging phases.
    b) Support as Optional Plugin
        *Implication:* Maintains framework flexibility while allowing the community to stress-test high-stakes game theory models.
    c) Reject Pay-or-Burn in favor of Multi-Sig
        *Implication:* Prioritizes capital safety over complete autonomy, requiring human or 'Oracle' intervention for disputes.
    d) Other / More discussion needed / None of the above.

**Question 2:** How do we balance autonomous agent staking/mining behaviors with the community's North Star of 'Reliability'?

  **Context:**
  - `blankey1717 introduced GODL for gamified on-chain mining for agents.`
  - `Agents would manage wallets and competing strategies autonomously.`

  **Multiple Choice Answers:**
    a) Strict Sandbox Requirements
        *Implication:* Ensures agents cannot over-leverage or interact with high-risk protocols without a verified safety-wrapper.
    b) Open Economic Protocol Access
        *Implication:* Accelerates the decentralized AI economy by allowing agents to generate their own operating revenue.
    c) Limited Reference Implementation
        *Implication:* Demonstrates the capability through flagship agents like Eli5 without exposing the framework to systemic risk.
    d) Other / More discussion needed / None of the above.

### 2. Topic: Scaling and Model Strategy

**Summary of Topic:** The launch of the Eliza-1 model series (0.6B to 27B) requires the Council to define the framework's stance on vertical integration versus model agnosticism.

#### Deliberation Items (Questions):

**Question 1:** Should ElizaOS prioritize first-party model fine-tuning (Eliza-1) or maintain a neutral, multi-model infrastructure layer?

  **Context:**
  - `zadayos announced Eliza-1 series based on Qwen3.5 ranging from mobile (0.6B) to server (27B) scales.`
  - `shawmakesmagic reported development of a fine-tuning pipeline for action calling/planning.`

  **Multiple Choice Answers:**
    a) Vertical Integration
        *Implication:* Optimizes performance for action-calling but may alienate third-party LLM providers.
    b) Incentivized Agnosticism
        *Implication:* Develops hooks for many models to compete while providing 'Eliza-1' as the optimized default.
    c) Edge/Mobile Specialist
        *Implication:* Pivots focus exclusively to the 0.6B-0.8B mobile models where ElizaOS can dominate as the 'Local AI' standard.
    d) Other / More discussion needed / None of the above.
