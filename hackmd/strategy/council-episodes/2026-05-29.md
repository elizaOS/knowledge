## 1) Episode Overview
Episodes covered:
- **RETRO-2026-04 — Monthly Retro: April 2026 (episode-retro-2026-04)**

April showed **real engineering momentum** (parallel action execution merged, ongoing database refactor, early safety/spend governance proposals, marketplace/payment/memory plugin exploration, early “one-click hosting” wins), but the council judged that **community confidence hit a floor** due to **token decline**, **onboarding/DX breakages**, and **perceived strategic drift**. The core takeaway: ElizaOS is now being evaluated as a **security + economics product**, not just a framework, and **reliability/DX** remains the adoption gate.

---

## 2) Key Strategic Themes
- **“Boring reliability” as the growth engine**
  - The council aligned that new capability is being discounted because first-run experiences and stability regressions are dominating perception.
  - Repeated mantra: *if `elizaos create` fails, nothing else matters.*

- **V2 stability vs V3 acceleration (avoid ecosystem fragmentation)**
  - Continued V3 work is justified, but leaving V2 integrators behind is actively eroding builder trust.
  - Need explicit **support windows** and a minimum **v2 LTS patch set** to prevent “abandonware” perception.

- **Economic autonomy requires security-by-default**
  - Identity, spend controls, and reputation are no longer “optional plugins later”; they are prerequisites for agents touching real money.
  - However, these must ship as **safe defaults** (fail-closed presets, reference patterns), not as optional complexity.

- **Token collapse → contributor retention risk**
  - Paying contributors in a rapidly declining token creates forced sell pressure and morale damage, accelerating a “death spiral” dynamic (less retention → slower shipping → worse narrative).

- **Security expectations rising (table stakes)**
  - Even without a major incident, the ecosystem expects formal security posture: vulnerability disclosure, dependency hygiene, supply-chain hardening.

- **Comms cadence + strategic clarity**
  - Confusion about “what ElizaOS is” versus adjacent experiments is compounding sentiment problems; clarity and predictable updates are treated as product features.

---

## 3) Important Decisions / Insights
- **Stop-the-line priority: Onboarding/DX stability**
  - Mac onboarding failures (notably **Bun postinstall behavior**) were called out as *blocking new developers at the first command*.
  - Decision posture: treat onboarding breakages as **P0 incidents** and prove fixes via CI + public visibility.

- **Formalize a V2 support policy (v2 LTS scope)**
  - Strategic position: V2 needs a defined minimal patch scope and support window so integrators can commit without fear of churn.

- **Define V3 release criteria and dates (avoid “endless refactor”)**
  - Publish a one-page roadmap including **alpha/beta targets**, **exit criteria** (test coverage, performance targets, migration guide), and rules for when V3 is considered ready.

- **Security baseline commitments**
  - Ship: **SECURITY.md**, responsible disclosure, automated dependency scanning/SBOM in CI.
  - Include at least one **fail-closed spend policy preset** (“off by exception”) as a default safety posture.

- **Spend governance must be a reference implementation, not lore**
  - Deliver a **safe default agent template** that developers can copy:
    - allowlisted actions
    - transaction simulation
    - risk checks (e.g., honeypot/rug heuristics)
    - documented extension points
  - Insight: safe economic behavior should be taught by example and enforced by defaults.

- **Compensation + transparency reset is a prerequisite to execution**
  - Council consensus: without stabilizing contributor incentives (e.g., partial stablecoin/hedged payouts), engineering priorities may be unstaffable regardless of technical direction.

- **Defer broad marketplace ambitions until the core path is trusted**
  - Explicitly deprioritize “big marketplace” pushes until install/reliability/security foundations are measurably stable.

## 4) Community Impact (elizaOS ecosystem)
- **Developers**
  - Clear signal that the near-term win condition is **deterministic onboarding** (especially macOS), fewer undocumented patterns, and a stable V2 lane.
  - A public “install health” posture reduces support burden and increases willingness to build.

- **Ecosystem builders / integrators**
  - A V2 LTS policy and V3 criteria reduce fragmentation risk and prevent integrators from being stranded between versions.

- **Security + economic users**
  - Treating spend governance/identity as core—shipped with fail-closed defaults—raises confidence for real-money agent deployments and reduces reputational tail risk.

- **Contributors**
  - Compensation volatility was identified as a direct threat to throughput and retention; a policy update is positioned as a “keep builders building” requirement.

- **Broader community / token holders**
  - The council emphasized that credibility recovery depends on **measurable shipping**, not narrative battles—weekly ship notes + monthly retros aim to dampen rumor-driven churn.
