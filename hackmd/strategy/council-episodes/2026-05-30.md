## 1) Episode Overview (2026-05-30)

Episodes covered:
- **RETRO-2026-04 — Monthly Retro: April 2026 (episode-retro-2026-04)**  
  April delivered real engineering momentum (parallel execution, runtime hardening, database refactor work, early spend-safety and marketplace/payment/memory plugin proposals), but **community sentiment hit a low** due to token decline, onboarding breakages, and perceived strategic drift. The council aligned on a **May “rebuild-the-floor” plan** centered on reliability, onboarding, security/spend governance defaults, and clearer roadmap + comms.

---

## 2) Key Strategic Themes

- **Reliability and Developer Experience (DX) as the growth gate**
  - “Capability shipping” is being discounted because **fresh installs and first-run workflows are failing**, especially on macOS (Bun postinstall).
  - The council treats `elizaos create` failures as **stop-the-line incidents**: without a boringly stable entry ramp, ecosystem adoption and trust collapse.

- **v2 stability vs. v3 acceleration (release discipline and support policy)**
  - Ongoing v3 work is justified, but **abandoning v2 integrators** (or appearing to) is fragmenting the builder ecosystem.
  - Need explicit **v2 support window (“v2 LTS”)** and **v3 exit criteria** to reduce roadmap ambiguity and churn.

- **Security + economics are now inseparable from the core framework**
  - Identity, spend controls, reputation, and “fail-closed” policies are no longer “nice-to-have plugins”; they are **prerequisites** for autonomous agents interacting with money.
  - Expectation shift: agent frameworks are increasingly perceived as **security products** (supply-chain hygiene, disclosure processes, safe defaults).

- **Token decline driving contributor retention risk**
  - Token-denominated compensation is producing **forced selling pressure**, morale damage, and risk of execution slowdown—creating a feedback loop (“death spiral”) that undermines shipping capacity.

- **Narrative coherence and communication cadence as trust infrastructure**
  - Confusion about what is “core ElizaOS” vs. “adjacent experiments” is amplifying distrust under bearish price action.
  - Strategy: fewer public “big ideas” until onboarding is stable; more **measurable, time-bound updates**.

---

## 3) Important Decisions / Insights

- **Prioritization decision for May (three-slot focus)**
  - The council converged on three immediate priorities:
    1. **DX / onboarding stability**
    2. **Roadmap clarity + v2 support policy**
    3. **Security + spend governance MVP (safe defaults)**
  - Explicit deferral: **broader marketplace ambitions** until core install/run/deploy path is stable and trusted.

- **DX determinism becomes a gating constraint for v3 credibility**
  - v3 progress will not translate into adoption unless the **developer entry ramp is deterministic** and reproducible in CI.

- **“Fail-closed” spend governance should be embodied as a reference template**
  - Spend controls must not be scattered across plugin lore; the council wants a **copyable agent template** that sets a safety baseline (allowlists, simulation, risk checks, documented extension points).

- **Security baselines formalization is table stakes**
  - Adopt **SECURITY.md**, responsible disclosure, automated dependency scanning/SBOM, and supply-chain hardening—before any major incident forces it.

- **Comms stance shift**
  - Commit to being explicitly human and accountable: **admit what broke**, publish the fix plan, and provide **verification artifacts** (install health dashboard/badge; weekly ship notes).

- **Compensation policy reset is treated as execution-critical**
  - Without a credible contributor compensation update (e.g., partial stable payouts/hedging), the council expects execution excellence to degrade regardless of architecture quality.

## 4) Community Impact (Broader elizaOS ecosystem)

- **Improved onboarding directly increases builder conversion and reduces support burden**
  - A measurable install health program (CI-reproduced macOS path + public dashboard) turns “trust through shipping” into **trust through reproducible success**.

- **Reduced ecosystem fragmentation**
  - A published **v2 LTS patch scope** and **v3 release criteria** protects integrators, plugins, and downstream products from being stranded during refactors.

- **Safer agent economy unlocks real-money use cases**
  - Shipping spend governance as defaults (fail-closed presets + simulation + risk checks) raises the baseline for responsible deployments and reduces the likelihood of catastrophic loss events that could damage the brand.

- **Stabilized contributor incentives helps preserve velocity**
  - A compensation policy update + transparent cadence supports contributor retention, sustaining PR throughput and issue resolution.

- **Clearer “North Star” reduces rumor-driven churn**
  - Reframing: **ElizaOS = agent framework + cloud platform**, with experiments positioned as downstream adopters (not competing missions), helps consolidate identity and community alignment.
