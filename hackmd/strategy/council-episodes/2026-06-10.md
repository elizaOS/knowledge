## 1) Episode Overview (2026-06-10)
- **RETRO-2026-04 — Monthly Retro: April 2026**
  - April delivered real technical momentum (parallel execution, database refactor progress, early agent economy/safety primitives), but hit a **community confidence low** driven by token decline, onboarding/DX failures, and perceived strategic drift.
  - Council aligned that **reliability + developer experience (DX)** and **economic/security defaults** are now inseparable from the core ElizaOS narrative.

---

## 2) Key Strategic Themes
- **“Boring reliability” as the primary growth lever**
  - New capabilities are being discounted because first-run and integrator experience remains inconsistent.
  - Council framing: *“If `elizaos create` fails, nothing else matters.”*

- **V2 stability vs. V3 acceleration (avoid ecosystem abandonment)**
  - V3 work is valid, but **v2 integrators feel stranded**, creating fragmentation and trust loss.
  - Need an explicit **v2 support/LTS policy** and clear **v3 release criteria**.

- **Economic autonomy requires security-by-default**
  - Identity, spend controls, reputation, and governance are no longer “optional plugins later.”
  - The council treats agent frameworks as **security products**: “safe defaults” must ship as paved paths.

- **Token collapse → contributor retention and execution risk**
  - Token-denominated compensation creates forced selling + morale damage → lower throughput → worse narrative (a “death spiral”).
  - Compensation stability is viewed as a prerequisite to sustaining engineering velocity.

- **Comms cadence and strategic clarity as product infrastructure**
  - Confusion over “what ElizaOS is” vs. adjacent experiments amplifies mistrust during downturns.
  - Weekly ship notes + monthly retros are positioned as trust-repair mechanisms.

---

## 3) Important Decisions / Insights (Outcomes & Positions)
- **Stop-the-line priority: onboarding determinism**
  - macOS (Apple Silicon) install failures and Bun postinstall behavior were treated as **blockers**—fixes should be reproducible in CI, not anecdotal.

- **Adopt a two-lane posture: stable vs. experimental**
  - The council reaffirmed: ship foundations, but don’t force builders to “guess the real branch.”
  - Implied requirement: **one stable lane** with health metrics and **explicit experimental work** clearly labeled.

- **Security baselines are now table stakes**
  - Even without a major incident, stakeholders expect:
    - formal vulnerability disclosure,
    - dependency hygiene/supply-chain hardening,
    - and default-safe configurations for economic actions.

- **Spend governance must be a reference implementation, not lore**
  - Direction: deliver a **copyable reference agent template** with allowlisted actions, simulation, and risk checks (fail-closed by default).

- **Defer broad marketplace ambition until core trust surfaces recover**
  - Council consensus: focus on DX stability + roadmap clarity + security/spend governance MVP; **postpone marketplace-scale narratives** until installs and trust are measurably stable.

---

## 4) Community Impact (Broader elizaOS Ecosystem)
- **Builders**
  - Clearer stable paths (v2 LTS + install health) reduce wasted time and lower support burden.
  - A defined v3 roadmap + migration expectations lowers integrator risk and ecosystem fragmentation.

- **Operators / production users**
  - Security defaults (SBOM/scanning + disclosure + spend fail-closed presets) raise confidence for real-money agent deployments.

- **Contributors**
  - Compensation policy reform (e.g., partial stable/hedged payouts) directly targets retention and sustained delivery capacity.

- **Community sentiment**
  - Regular, measurable updates (“receipts”) are expected to reverse rumor-driven churn and restore “Trust Through Shipping” credibility.

- **Onboarding & DX stabilization (v2 LTS patch set)**
  - Fix macOS/Bun postinstall blockers; make create→run deterministic.
  - Stand up **Install Health CI** and a public **Install Health dashboard** (OS/runtime matrix).
  - Target: **~80% reduction** in fresh-install failure reports.

- **Publish v3 roadmap + v2 support policy**
  - One-page roadmap with **alpha/beta dates**, **exit criteria** (test coverage, perf, migration guide), and a **v2 support window** with defined patch scope.

  - Add **SECURITY.md** and a responsible disclosure process.
  - Add automated dependency scanning + **SBOM** generation in CI.
  - Ship at least one **fail-closed spend policy preset** (“off by exception”).

    - allowlisted actions,

    - **ElizaOS = agent framework + cloud platform**
