## Episode Overview (2026-06-06)
Episodes covered:
- **RETRO-2026-04 — Monthly Retro: April 2026** (*episode-retro-2026-04*)
- **RETRO-2026-05 — Monthly Retro: May 2026** (*episode-retro-2026-05*)

Across April–May, the council aligned on a single overriding thesis: **reliability and developer experience (DX) are the growth bottleneck**, and **security + economic safety are now inseparable from the core framework narrative**. Engineering momentum remained strong (parallel execution, runtime hardening, vault/secrets, ESM modernization), but **trust and adoption were gated by onboarding failures, unclear release trains (v2 vs v3), plugin supply-chain fragility, and contributor incentive instability**.

---

## Key Strategic Themes
- **Reliability as the product (not an aspiration)**
  - “If `elizaos create` fails, nothing else matters.” First-run success is treated as a stop-the-line incident.
  - Shift toward defining reliability via measurable outcomes: install success, CI reproducibility, message delivery guarantees, crash-free runs, and long-lived agent integrity.

- **DX determinism + a “Known Good Stack”**
  - Persistent onboarding breakpoints (e.g., macOS/Bun postinstall) are seen as existential to developer adoption.
  - The council emphasized reducing “works on my machine” variance via CI, dashboards, and opinionated golden paths.

- **Security + economic autonomy converging**
  - Agents interacting with money require **safe defaults**: fail-closed spend policies, allowlists, simulation, audit trails, and responsible disclosure.
  - Security posture is elevated from optional hardening to baseline expectations (SECURITY.md, dependency scanning, SBOM, supply-chain controls).

- **Plugin ecosystem scaling constraints**
  - ESM-first modernization and plugin growth are necessary, but **registry uptime and distribution reliability** are gating ecosystem health.
  - “Registry 404s are ecosystem killers” became a central operational insight.

- **Long-lived agent “memory rot” as the next reliability frontier**
  - Reliability must hold over months, not minutes; “memory rot” after ~90 days was flagged as a strategic risk requiring measurement harnesses and mitigation primitives.

- **Trust, narrative clarity, and comms cadence**
  - Community sentiment degraded due to token decline, perceived drift, missing release notes, and ambiguous “what is official.”
  - Council consensus: fewer big public ideas until onboarding is boringly stable; ship notes and retros must become a ritual.

- **Contributor incentives and sustainability**
  - Token-denominated contributor compensation under high volatility was recognized as a retention and execution risk.
  - Bus factor/maintainer concentration is treated as a decentralization credibility issue and an operational risk.

---

## Important Decisions / Insights
- **April operating system (top priorities locked)**
  - **DX stability first**, **roadmap/support policy second**, and **security/spend governance MVP third**.
  - Explicit deferral: **broad marketplace ambitions** until the core install/run path is stable and trusted.

- **V2 vs V3: stop stranding integrators**
  - A defined **v2 LTS-like support window** and patch scope is required to protect ecosystem builders while v3 evolves.
  - v3 needs **published alpha/beta milestones and exit criteria** (test coverage, perf targets, migration guide) to avoid “endless refactor” perception.

- **Security baselines move from “later” to “now”**
  - Adopt **SECURITY.md + responsible disclosure**, automated dependency scanning/SBOM, and ship **at least one fail-closed spend preset**.

- **Spend governance must be a reference path, not plugin lore**
  - Deliver a **reference agent template** that embodies safe-by-default economic actions:
    - allowlisted actions
    - transaction simulation
    - risk checks (honeypot/rug heuristics)
    - clear extension points

- **May focus refines the reliability program**
  - Treat Telegram message loss and registry failures as **product-level failures** with explicit SLAs and test harnesses.
  - Begin a **Memory Rot Program** with a published “90-day longevity harness” and at least one mitigation that measurably reduces drift.

- **Operational clarity is part of shipping**
  - Missing/unclear release notes are framed as a trust failure, not a cosmetic issue.
  - “One-page plan” style comms with metrics, owners placeholders, and verification mechanisms (dashboards/badges) is preferred.

## Community Impact (elizaOS ecosystem)
- **Developers**
  - Near-term: improved confidence hinges on a reproducible install path (especially macOS), a stable v2 lane, and registry reliability.
  - Medium-term: clearer release trains and migration guidance reduce ecosystem fragmentation and plugin breakage churn.

- **Plugin authors and integrators**
  - Will benefit from a stable compatibility contract, better registry uptime, and ESM migration support—reducing support overhead and increasing adoption of third-party work.

- **Agent builders operating with real money**
  - Safer defaults and spend governance references reduce risk of wallet-drain patterns and “unsafe agent” reputational blowups.
  - Auditability (receipts, policy engines, disclosure processes) becomes a baseline expectation for serious deployments.

- **Contributors and maintainers**
  - Compensation policy instability and bus factor concentration are direct threats to execution capacity.
  - Publishing ownership maps/CODEOWNERS and reducing PR review latency are positioned as ecosystem scaling levers.

- **Broader community sentiment**
  - The council’s posture is explicitly anti-“death spiral”:
    - regain trust via boring reliability
    - communicate predictably
    - stop surprising users with breakages or ambiguous “official vs experimental” boundaries
