## 3) Strategic Prioritization (Impact × Risk × Dependencies)

### Top initiatives to prioritize (next 7–14 days)

1) **Ship a v2.x “Activation Kit” (Docs + Demo)**
- **User impact:** Very high (unblocks onboarding + reduces repeated Discord questions).
- **Technical risk:** Low.
- **Dependencies:** none; can be parallelized across 1–2 contributors + community helpers.
- **Deliverables (minimum viable):**
  - v2.0/v2.0.2 **release notes summary** (what changed, why native-app integration matters, migration notes)
  - “**Getting started in 15 minutes**” guide (install → run sample agent → enable 1 connector → first success criteria)
  - **One canonical demo video** (5–10 minutes) + 1 longer walkthrough (30–45 minutes)
- **Success metric:** reduce repeated onboarding questions by **≥50%** week-over-week; increase “first run success” confirmations in Discord.

2) **Codify deployment guidance for real-time voice + GPU**
- **User impact:** High for builder segment; improves perceived production readiness.
- **Technical risk:** Medium (must be accurate across environments).
- **Critical path dependency:** local inference/runtime assumptions; GPU access docs.
- **Deliverables:**
  - Reference **docker-compose** architecture: inference, API, DB, queue/worker, front-end (explicitly non-monolithic)
  - GPU passthrough recipes (NVIDIA/AMD where supported), storage, and latency checklist
  - “When *not* to do single-container” decision guide
- **Success metric:** at least **1 community implementation** reaches stable multi-container local deployment following docs, reported back.

3) **Answer/close open narrative loops (reduce uncertainty debt)**
- **User impact:** Medium; protects trust and reduces rumor surface.
- **Technical risk:** Low.
- **Items:**
  - Clarify “AI agent status” in one pinned post (what is “released” vs “in progress,” what quality bar to expect)
  - Hardware: explicitly state whether “physical e1 chip” is planned, experimental, or unknown
  - Token/governance: maintain a single canonical FAQ link to avoid repeated, inconsistent answers
- **Success metric:** fewer repeated “status” questions; more links to canonical FAQ in community replies.
