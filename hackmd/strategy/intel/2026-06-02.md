### Priority 2 — DeFi Integration Strategy: “Abstraction-first with escape hatches” (High impact, medium risk)
**Decision needed:** Whether to formalize Smeltor’s multi-protocol abstraction as an Eliza plugin/framework pattern, while still enabling Kamino-first GTM.

**Recommendation (architecture):**
- Adopt a **two-layer model**:
  1) **DeFi Action Surface (stable interface):** swap/earn/borrow/perps/bridge returning **signable calldata** (non-custodial).
  2) **Protocol Adapters (pluggable):** Kamino, Aave, Morpho, Compound, Hyperliquid modules with:
     - capability discovery
     - chain/protocol constraints
     - “quirks” handled locally
- Add **escape hatches**: allow callers to request:
  - “raw” protocol calldata
  - explicit routing (force Kamino vs best-route)
  - partial fills / slippage / leverage constraints where supported

**Go-to-market path (pragmatic):**
- Ship **Kamino adapter first** (meets partner-attention goal), but inside the abstraction scaffolding so it doesn’t become a dead-end bespoke integration.

**Dependencies / risks:**
- Needs a clear plugin boundary in ElizaOS (how actions are registered, contexts, planner ranking).
- Risk of over-generalizing early: mitigate by shipping Kamino-first with a spec that accommodates future protocols.

**Resourcing:** 1 senior engineer (interface + planner integration) + 1 engineer (Kamino adapter) for 2–3 weeks to first usable release.
