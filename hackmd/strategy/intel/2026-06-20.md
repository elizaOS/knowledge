## 3) Strategic Prioritization (impact vs risk, dependencies, resourcing)

### Initiative evaluation (next 2–4 weeks)

#### A) Reliability: “Cloud agents should never fail due to shared DB contention”
- **User impact:** Very high (prevents outages; improves perceived maturity).
- **Technical risk:** Medium (data migration, persistence semantics).
- **Dependency notes:** PR #8754 shifts new agents to PGlite; ensure:
  - clear policy for existing agents (migrate vs grandfather);
  - backup/restore + export paths for PGlite state;
  - observability for per-agent DB health.
- **Recommendation:** Continue investing; this is a prerequisite for consumer UX work to “stick.”

#### B) Consumer UX packaging: “No-code” as a guided product, not a capability claim
- **User impact:** High (bridges “value prop unclear”).
- **Technical risk:** Medium-low (mostly UX + templates + docs).
- **Critical dependency:** A crisp positioning + onboarding narrative (“Eliza OS” / “OS for agents”) and a template library tied to common outcomes.
- **Recommendation:** Allocate a dedicated “Activation” squad (PM/Design/Eng) to ship 3–5 guided flows.

#### C) Trust & governance response (financial allegations + scams)
- **User impact:** Very high (reduces reputational damage).
- **Technical risk:** Low (comms/process), but **execution risk** high if slow or vague.
- **Dependency:** internal legal/finance alignment; moderation tooling.
- **Recommendation:** Time-box an official response path; improve channel anti-scam operations.

#### D) Hyperforge status clarity
- **User impact:** Medium (reduces uncertainty).
- **Technical risk:** Low.
- **Recommendation:** Publish a short status update (active / paused / merged into other work), plus next milestone.
