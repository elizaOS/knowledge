# Fact Briefing: 2026-06-23

## Overall Summary
Development activity on June 23 focused on core infrastructure stability and cloud job recovery, following a week marked by the successful production launch of the Eliza Cloud platform. Community discussions centered on token liquidity plans and clarifying the branding of the project as an operating system rather than a cloud service.

## Key Facts

- The Eliza Cloud platform successfully transitioned from staging to production, verified by a 10-user launch tracker in issue #8434.
- PR #9072 was initiated to adjust stale-job recovery sizing to prevent provision flapping during cold-boot scenarios.
- The core developer odilitime announced the launch of Ruby Trivia to the community on June 22.
- Database architecture in elizaos/eliza was overhauled to adopt in-container PGlite databases to improve connection scalability.
- Community member .chomppp inquired about Solana liquidity plans, but core developers clarified the foundation manages the token independently.
- Large token movements (1.02B to 0) from Shaw's dormant wallet were flagged by community members on June 22 as a point of concern.
- Developers implemented fallback mechanisms for direct-reply requests in PR #9074 to improve system reliability.
- The project positioning was clarified to emphasize 'Eliza OS' as the preferred naming convention over 'eliza cloud' to reflect its nature as an AI agent operating system.
