# ElizaOS Weekly Newsletter (Jun 14–Jun 20, 2026)

## 1) Executive Summary

This week was all about **shipping stability** and **making Eliza Cloud/App hosting feel real**—not just promising.

- **Eliza Cloud moved from staging to production** (per the weekly tracker), marking a big milestone in commercial readiness and operational confidence. The team focused on hardening the “money path” (credits, billing, shutdown behavior) and eliminating production footguns.
- **A major scalability incident was addressed head-on:** the dreaded Postgres `"too many clients"` failure mode was mitigated by moving **new managed agents to a local in-container PGlite DB**, dramatically reducing shared DB pressure and improving reliability at scale.
- **Mobile/desktop experience continued to converge** with fixes that unblock mobile builds and improve onboarding and chat consistency—key for expanding beyond “power users.”

## 2) Development Updates (Technical Highlights)

### Cloud reliability & scalability
- **In-container PGlite DB for managed agents (new default for managed agents):** A core fix for connection pooling pressure—managed agents now keep state locally in their container via PGlite, avoiding the shared Postgres pool bottleneck. This directly targets the production `"too many clients"` failure mode and should improve both stability and horizontal scaling.
- **Lean chat plugins integrated for compatibility:** Alongside the PGlite shift, the team integrated lean chat plugins to ensure managed agents remain compatible with the updated architecture and boot faster with fewer unnecessary components.
- **Cloud gateway and infra hardening continued:** Recent changes (from the weekly summary) also included upgrades like moving the network gateway toward a **Cloudflare Worker model** and tightening workflows to make CI/deploy more reliable.

### Billing, credits, and “production correctness”
- Multiple efforts across the week emphasized **billing accuracy** and preventing “service leaks” (agents/apps continuing to run when they shouldn’t, credits not decrementing correctly, or double-billing on cron retries). This work is foundational: it protects both users (correct charges) and the platform (no unbounded infra burn).

### Mobile parity & onboarding improvements
- The team resolved **long-standing mobile build issues** to ensure Android/iOS builds reliably include the required web assets and configuration, bringing mobile closer to desktop feature parity.
- Onboarding and chat UX improvements continued, including simplifying sign-up/runtime selection and standardizing chat surfaces so users can interact consistently across devices.

## 3) Community Spotlight (Discord)

A few conversations stood out this week because they surfaced **product clarity gaps** and **ecosystem trust expectations**—the kind of feedback that’s most valuable early.

### “What is waifu.fun, who is it for, and why should I use it?”
- **ghss44** raised direct questions about **waifu.fun’s purpose**, target users, and what it means to “launch trading agents” there. Notably, these questions went largely unanswered in-thread, which signals a real documentation/positioning gap—not just confusion from new users.
- **odilitime** (core dev/mod) was candid: they didn’t build waifu.fun and are **still learning the platform**, planning to launch a personal agent this week to understand it better. That transparency is helpful—but it also reinforces the need for a clear, centralized explanation.

### Branding & usability: “Eliza OS” vs “Eliza Cloud”
- ghss44 also argued for stronger positioning as **“Eliza OS: the operating system for AI agents”**, with a bigger emphasis on **everyday users** (no-code, button-first flows, simple agent creation). Another community member countered that Eliza Cloud already offers no-code creation/deployment—suggesting the capability may exist, but the **perceived UX/story** still isn’t landing for everyone.

### Moderation & safety
- A scammer was reported and handled by moderators. No drama, just a reminder: keeping channels clean is ongoing work as the ecosystem grows.

### Governance & rumor management
- A heated discussion included **financial allegations** involving Eliza Foundation and a third party, plus broader commentary about lawsuits and token events. These were presented as claims in chat, not verified facts. The takeaway: the community wants **clear, official communication** when serious allegations circulate, even if the response is simply “we’re reviewing; here’s what we can share.”

## 4) Token Economics (AI16z token + auto.fun/waifu.fun)

There weren’t formal, published tokenomics releases in the provided data this week—but there *was* strong signal from community discussion:

- **Token fragmentation concerns:** **zadayos** questioned why **waifu.fun appears to use a separate token** instead of consolidating around **$ELIZAOS**, warning this can create dilution and skepticism. This question remained unanswered publicly in the thread.
- **Legacy token confusion still lingers:** Community members referenced the historic turbulence where an earlier **$eliza** token reportedly crashed after a new **$ELIZA** token launch. Regardless of specifics, it’s clear many holders want a simple, consistent narrative.

**What would help next:** a short “one-pager” that explains:
1) the relationship between **$ELIZAOS**, waifu.fun, auto.fun (if applicable), and any other ecosystem tokens,  
2) the utility and governance expectations for each, and  
3) whether consolidation is planned, possible, or explicitly not.
